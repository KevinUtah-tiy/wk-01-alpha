Types of Junipers

Junipers are evergreen conifers with either needle-like or scale-like leaves.


Juniperus osteosperma

The Utah Juniper is listed as a shrub or small tree.

  • native: southwestern United States
  • height: 3 to 6 meters (10 to 20 feet)
  • seeds: single
  • leaves: whorls of three, 1-2mm long, 1-1.5mm wide
  • fruit: berry-like cones, blue-brown (8-13mm diameter)

Juniperus communis

The common juniper is listed as a shrub or small tree.

  • height: from a shrub to 16 meters (50 feet)
  • seeds: single
  • leaves: needle-like, whorls of three
  • fruit: berry-like cones, purple-black, spherical (4-12mm diameter)

Juniperus chinensis

The Chinese Juniper, also known as bonsai juniper, is listed as a shrub or small tree.

  • native: northeast Asia
  • height: 1-20 meters (66 feet)
  • seeds: 2 to 4
  • leaves: scale-like, 1.5-3mm long
  • fruit: berry-like, blue-black, spherical (7-12mm diameter)

Juniperus oxycedrus

The Western Prickly Juniper is listed as a shrub or small tree.

  • native: Mediterranean region of Europe
  • height: shrub 2-3m tall to a tree 10-15m tall (6.5-10 ft and 33-49 ft, respectively)
  • seeds: single
  • leaves: needle-like, whorls of three, 5-20mm long
  • fruit: berry-like, green to orange-red, spherical (7-12mm diameter)

Juniperus virginiana

The Eastern Redcedar is a dense tree with reddish-brown bark.

  • native: from the Great Plains of North America to the east, from the Gulf of Mexico to southeastern Canada.
  • height: 5-20 meters (16-66 feet)
  • seeds: one to three
  • leaves: scale-like, whorls of three, 2-4mm long
  • fruit: berry-like, dark purple-blue, 2-3mm long, 1.5mm wide
